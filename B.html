Hadley Leggett

Upmarket Women's Fiction

Every story changes based on who’s telling it.
I write multi-layered, multiple-perspective novels that explore truth in shades of gray.
My first novel, A BETTER MOTHER, tells the story of three women fighting for custody of the same two little girls. Bio mom, foster mom, and estranged grandmother each think they know what's best, until the truth proves too complicated for any of them to face alone.
My second project, SPLIT OPEN THE WORLD, is a dual-timeline, dual-POV story about a celebrity couples’ therapist who's desperate to save his own marriage—while his wife is equally desperate to get out. The husband’s story begins fifteen years in the past, when he met Nora and fell in love; her story starts at the moment she realizes she wants to leave.
You can find my short stories and essays at The Bellevue Literary Review, Literary Mama, Halfway Down the Stairs, and 50-Word Stories. I’m also a medical doctor and science journalist, and you can find my science writing at WIRED.com
I'm a fiction reader at Longleaf Review and a beta reader for Spun Yarn. My first manuscript was chosen for mentorship through the AWP Writer-to-Writer program and Author Mentor Match.

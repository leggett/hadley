Hadley Leggett

Writer, reader, mother of three.

Perhaps I should have realized I wasn’t cut out for medicine on my first day of anatomy lab, when I sat down on a wooden crate and discovered my seat was filled with hemisected human heads. I nearly passed out that afternoon, and also about 37 more times before finishing med school.
I'd fallen in love with storytelling as a child, but as a people-pleaser born into a family of scientists, it never occured to me I could become a writer. That would be like aspiring to be a Hollywood actress, or a mermaid-unicorn-fairy princess.
After leaving medicine, it took me ten more years—and another graduate program, this time in science journalism—to learn to trust myself and my writing dreams. When I enrolled in my first fiction workshop at Hugo House, I knew I’d found my home.
Writer
My short stories and essays have appeared in Bellevue Literary Review, Literary Mama, Halfway Down the Stairs, and 50-Word Stories, and you can find my science writing at WIRED.com. In 2020, I was selected for the AWP Writer-to-Writer mentorship program and Author Mentor Match.
My first novel manuscript won the 2021 Rising Star Award from the Women's Fiction Writers Association. ALL THEY ASK IS EVERYTHING tells the story of three women all determined to be the perfect mom—for the same two little girls. Accusations fly as bio mom, foster mom, and estranged grandmother vie for custody, until the truth shatters everything they thought they knew about motherhood.
Now that I’m finishing a draft of my second novel, I’ve discovered a common thread in my writing: I’m fascinated by the way every story changes based on who’s telling it. As a result, I write multi-layered, multiple-perspective novels that explore truth in shades of grey.
I'm represented by Serene Hakim at Ayesha Pande Literary.
Reader
Without an MFA, I’ve found my literary education within the pages of my favorite novels. Some of my favorite authors include Celeste Ng, Brit Bennett, Tana French, Madeline Miller, Curtis Sittenfeld, Anthony Doerr, and Liane Moriarty. I’m a subscriber of way too many literary magazines, and I work as a fiction reader for Longleaf Review and as a beta reader for Spun Yarn.
Mother of three
Mothering breaks me down and builds me up in ways I never expected, and my role as a mom is integral to my life as a writer. I wrote the rough draft of my first novel in three-hour chunks, while my youngest daughter was in preschool, and these days I squeeze in revisions between packing lunches and folding laundry. But my kids keep me sane through the inevitable stream of rejections and disappointments in my writing life. And whenever I start to take myself too seriously, I go build a Lego castle.
